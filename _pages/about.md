I am a graduate researcher at the [Intelligence Networking Lab](http://www.cbchae.org/), Yonsei University, advised by Prof. Chan-Byoung Chae. My research focuses on **next-generation (6G) wireless communication systems**, with particular emphasis on hardware-aware and measurement-driven system design.

My current research interests include:

- **Fluid Antenna Systems (FAS)** — especially pixel-based reconfigurable antennas (PRA-FAS), port selection, and channel estimation under sparse channel conditions
- **Tri-Hybrid MIMO architectures** — combining digital, analog, and antenna-domain (EM) processing for scalable spatial degrees of freedom
- **SDR-based testbed validation** — bridging the gap between theoretical channel models and measured over-the-air performance

Before graduate school, I built a background in financial modeling and data analysis, which shapes my quantitative, evidence-first approach to wireless systems research.

Feel free to reach out if you are interested in FAS, reconfigurable antennas, or 6G MIMO research.
